# Code of Conduct

This repository exists to help people who have lost something — an account, a community, years of content, a verified identity, or a piece of their digital life — recover it. Many of the people who interact with this repo are in distress.

## Our standards

When contributing, reviewing, or interacting in this repo:

- **Be respectful.** People filing AG complaints and writing demand letters are not in a great mood. Cut them slack.
- **Be specific.** Vague advice doesn't help. Concrete, dated, sourced information does.
- **Be honest.** Don't oversell what works. Don't promise outcomes the framework cannot deliver.
- **Stay on-topic.** This isn't a place for political commentary about Big Tech, debates about platform policies, or general venting. Issues about specific recovery problems are welcome; threads about how social media is bad are not.

## What we don't tolerate

- Harassment of any kind
- Personal attacks on contributors, users, or platform employees
- Identifying information about specific people (employees, regulators, case participants) shared without consent
- Promotion of paid recovery services, "hackers for hire," or anyone claiming to guarantee account recovery
- Encouragement of fraud, identity theft, or circumventing legitimate platform decisions
- Malware, phishing, or any actively harmful contribution

## Enforcement

Repo maintainers handle enforcement. Violations may result in:
- Edited or removed comments / PRs
- Closed issues without explanation
- Banning from the repo for repeat or severe violations

Contact maintainers via the issue tracker for any concerns.

## A note for users in crisis

If you're using this skill because you just lost something and you're not okay: the skill is here for the technical work, but the emotional weight of losing a community or a years-old account is real. Please take care of yourself while you work through this.

If you're in genuine emotional crisis, this repo is not a substitute for human support. Reach out to friends, family, or a mental health professional. The account will still be here when you're ready to file the next form.
