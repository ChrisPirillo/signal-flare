## Known organizational gaps

- **Gap 1: Account limitations vs. account holds.** "Limitations" restrict functionality but maintain access; "holds" may freeze funds. The two have different appeal paths.
- **Gap 2: Frozen funds during disputes.** PayPal can hold funds for 180 days during disputes; release process is opaque.
- **Gap 3: Risk team decisions are often automated.** Initial review by automation; human review only on escalation.

---

## CFPB and unique regulatory pathway

PayPal, unlike pure social platforms, is regulated as a financial services provider. This means **the CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau) is the most powerful regulator for PayPal disputes**, more so than state AGs.

- **CFPB complaint portal:** https://www.consumerfinance.gov/complaint/
- **CFPB-specific framing:** Much stronger lever than for non-financial platforms

For PayPal-specific cases, **add the CFPB to your Stage 3 escalation** in addition to the state AG and FTC.

## Anti-patterns specific to PayPal

- **Don't dispute via card issuer if you've also opened a PayPal dispute.** Creates conflict.
- **Don't make new transactions during a limitation review.** Adds noise.
- **Don't withdraw funds in unusual ways during disputes.**
