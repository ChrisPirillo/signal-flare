## Known organizational gaps

- **Gap 1: LinkedIn vs. Microsoft account integration.** Accounts linked to Microsoft accounts have additional complexity in recovery.
- **Gap 2: Restriction-vs-removal distinction unclear.** "Restricted" accounts have limited functionality but unclear path to full restoration.
- **Gap 3: Identity verification can fail for legitimate users.** Especially affects users without standard government ID formats.

---

## Documented patterns of failure

- **Mass automated restrictions** affecting active users
- **Restoration delays** of weeks to months
- **Appeals routing back to similar automated review**

## Public-pressure channels

- **@LinkedInHelp on X**

## Anti-patterns specific to LinkedIn

- **Don't connect with strangers en masse.** Triggers automation that can restrict accounts.
- **Don't use LinkedIn for non-business purposes that could trigger ToS issues.**
- **Don't create duplicate accounts for "professional vs personal."** Single-profile-per-person rule.

---

## Platform-specific harm narrative angles

- Loss of professional network built over years
- Loss of recommendations/endorsements
- Loss of job-search tools (especially during job transitions)
- Loss of business/recruiter access
- Loss of professional reputation associated with verified profile
