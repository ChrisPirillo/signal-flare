## Known organizational gaps

- **Gap 1: VAC bans are notoriously irreversible.** Valve's automated anti-cheat (VAC) bans are stated as final with no appeals process.
- **Gap 2: Purchase disputes via Steam vs. card issuer.** Steam discourages chargebacks; chargebacks often result in account locking.
- **Gap 3: Trade and item transfer restrictions.** Recovered hijacked accounts often have items/trades that cannot be reversed.

---

## Documented patterns of failure

- **Account hijacking for trade theft** — major pattern, especially for accounts with valuable inventory
- **VAC ban appeals failure**
- **Slow recovery for accounts with no purchase history (gift accounts)**

## Anti-patterns specific to Steam

- **Don't issue chargebacks.** Steam locks accounts with chargebacks.
- **Don't attempt to recover via third-party Steam recovery services.** Scams.
- **Don't accept "Steam Support" DMs in Steam chat.** Always scams.

---

## Platform-specific harm narrative angles

- Loss of game library (often hundreds to thousands of dollars)
- Loss of in-game items and Steam Market inventory
- Loss of Steam wallet balance
- Loss of trade history and friends list
