# Precedents

Anonymized success stories from people this framework has worked for. These exist to (a) calibrate expectations for new readers and (b) refine the framework based on what actually works.

To contribute, see the format in [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md#documenting-a-success-story).

**Privacy rules — strictly enforced:**
- No names, no usernames, no specific identifying details
- No platform employee names
- No other parties' identifying info
- No specific months or years more granular than necessary (use ranges like "12+ months" or "in the past two years")
- No state names — use "a US state" or, where relevant, "a state with an active consumer protection AG"
- Just the pattern and the outcome

If your case can be matched to you by anyone who knows your situation, it's not anonymized enough.

---

## Format

```markdown
### [Platform category], [Scenario type], [Approximate timeframe range]

- Initial issue: [1 sentence, no identifying details]
- Time stuck in standard support: [duration as a range — "12+ months", "several weeks", etc.]
- Stages used: [which of 0–5 you ran]
- What worked: [the specific channel that produced the resolution]
- Time from that channel to resolution: [duration]
- Final outcome: [what was restored, in generic terms]
```

## Cases

### Major social platform, wrongful disable after retracted third-party claims

- Initial issue: Personal account disabled after automated enforcement of third-party claims that were subsequently retracted by the original claimant.
- Time stuck in standard support: 12+ months. Multiple appeals through the platform's standard process produced only automated rejections.
- Stages used: 0, 1, 2, 3 (state Attorney General complaint only — did not need FTC, congressional, or attorney letter)
- What worked: State Attorney General consumer complaint
- Time from that channel to resolution: approximately 3 weeks from when the AG forwarded the complaint to the platform
- Final outcome: Account silently restored. No personal communication from the platform. No financial recovery sought (no monetized assets at stake in this case).

This pattern — extended support failure → state AG complaint → silent restoration in 3–4 weeks — has appeared across multiple platforms and case types. Long support failure, fast resolution after regulatory pressure.

## How to contribute your story

If the skill helped you recover an account, please consider adding your case here. The pattern is more useful than the specifics.

Submit a PR with your case appended to the "Cases" section above using the format at the top of this file. Strip every identifying detail — re-read your contribution and ask: "Could someone who knows my situation match this case to me?" If yes, abstract further.

Focus on:
- What scenario type
- How long you were stuck before escalation (in a range)
- Which stage produced the resolution
- How long resolution took once that stage was activated
- What you actually got back (in generic terms)

Your case helps calibrate the next person's expectations and decisions. It is the most useful single contribution you can make to this repo.
